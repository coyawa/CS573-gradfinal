In most states, the winner of the popular vote gets all of the state's electoral votes. Maine and Nebraska award one electoral vote to the winner of the popular vote in each congressional district and two electoral votes to the statewide winner. The model used here allocates the congressional district electoral votes in those states separately from the statewide electoral votes.
